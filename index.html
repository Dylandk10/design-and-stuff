BIO

My career in software development first began as a young teenager, around the age of thirteen. My friends and I wanted to design a game based off our favorite game RuneScape. This little adventure introduced me to the world of programming, and I fell in love instantly. Soon after, my research had me exploring virtual private networks and hosting applications. Fast forward to my college days, I studied Computer Information Systems at Towson University, where I was a dean list student.

I loved academics and became obsessed with two classes in particular, discrete mathematics and data structures. Through my college days I found my favorite area of programming was backend development. I built multiple fully functional web-apps with my college friends and hosted them for a short while. While pursuing these personal projects, I also landed my first internship as a web developer. I was tasked with building the front-end website using React.js and jQuery.

Following my first internship, I landed my second internship shortly after. This second internship would take me out of my comfort zone of web-development and would take me into the world of automation development. Though I found automation to be a very fun and challenging part of software engineering, I wanted to continue my career on the web side. For the past two years I have been working as a full stack developer and DevOps engineer. Though my passion for writing code and building software has not and most likely will not ever die, I found a new passion inside of me. This passion was technical writing. I love writing code but explaining software and how it works is a much different challenge. A challenge I really enjoy taking head on. Technical writing allows me to use both my passions for software development and writing together. I get to explain complex software requirements and user experience, while focusing on the language and grammar used to explain these technologies. Software and writing are two things I find to be the most fulfilling.
